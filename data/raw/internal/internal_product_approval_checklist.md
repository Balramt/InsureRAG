# Internal Product Approval Checklist

## Section 1: Purpose

This checklist defines the minimum internal approval steps before a new insurance product is launched or an existing insurance product is materially changed.

The checklist supports consistent product governance, regulatory alignment, and documentation of product approval decisions.

## Section 2: Scope

This checklist applies to insurance products launched or modified for the German market.

It should be used by product managers, compliance teams, legal teams, risk teams, data protection teams, and distribution governance teams.

## Section 3: Required Product Documents

Before launch, the product manager must prepare the following documents:

- product description,
- target market definition,
- customer information package,
- risk assessment,
- distribution plan,
- pricing and underwriting summary,
- claims handling overview,
- data protection assessment,
- compliance sign-off record.

## Section 4: Compliance and Legal Review

The compliance team must verify whether the product documentation is aligned with applicable German insurance law, EU regulatory requirements, and internal company policies.

The legal team must review unclear legal interpretations, high-risk product features, or conflicts between internal policy and external regulation.

## Section 5: AI and Data Use Review

If artificial intelligence, machine learning, automated decision-making, or customer profiling is used in the product lifecycle, the product team must request an additional AI governance and data protection review.

This review should consider model purpose, input data, explainability, fairness, customer impact, monitoring, and escalation procedures.

## Section 6: Approval Decision

A product must not be launched until all mandatory reviews are completed and documented.

The approval record should include the final decision, review comments, unresolved risks, responsible approvers, approval date, and required follow-up actions.

## Section 7: Escalation

If a product requirement is unclear, incomplete, or potentially inconsistent with external regulation, the product manager must escalate the issue to compliance or legal before launch.

High-risk issues must be documented in the approval record.
